The Gathering

One of Gather’s objectives is to encourage and develop local speaking talent. The keynote session is our only conference track, where three hand-picked speakers will be presenting in the main auditorium.

These speakers are industry leaders whom we personally admire and we’re quite excited to have them for Gather. This year, they are: Ben Gracewood, Sacha Judd, and Philip Fierlinger.

Ben Gracewood

Ben is the practice lead at Marker Metro with a long history in leading tech companies. He thinks the New Zealand government can do better at procuring IT services.

Sacha Judd

Sacha is our favourite partner at Buddle Findlay and assists some of New Zealand's biggest tech startups. She shares fond memories of her first computer and how you can code for fun and profit.

Philip Fierlinger

As Head of Design at Xero, Philip has created a true game changer. He believes design is how you invent the future and he has proof.

The Sessions

During the rest of the day, we focus on session streams that are divided into multiple 40-minute blocks.

You have free rein to do absolutely anything you want. They can be show-and-tells, discussions, lessons, or even just a sit-down-and-make-stuff.

You don’t have to be the expert to host a session. Suggest a topic and see if anyone is interested in helping you run it. Sessions are not meant to be a series of lectures – everyone is encouraged to participate.

What is Angry Client?

Barcamp Auckland 2 featured a game called ‘Angry Client’. It was so much fun, we’re bringing it back as a side activity this year! Watch as teams race against the clock to build a website to please Lance Wiggs the Angry Client and win the Angry Client Trophy.

How does Angry Client work?

Angry Client is a rapid development competition for teams of 1–3 people. You’ll have 45 minutes to complete a short web development task and win the Angry Client over. The main point of Angry Client is to make the Angry Client happy have fun and taunt the Angry Client.

How do I participate?

Simply register your team and turn up on the day. Easy.

Who should be on my team?

Anyone you like! However, you might want to ensure you cover a mix of the following skills:

  • Developer/programmer/coder
  • Content creator/copywriter
  • UI/UX
  • Project manager/client wrangler
  • Claymation Modeller
  • Playdough eater
  • Tinsel applier
  • Tester

What will we be doing?

The brief will be provided to you at the start of the competition and you’ll have 45 minutes to build the solution. You can use any kind of code: HTML, JavaScript, PHP, Ruby, Python, Crayon, Chopsticks, Playdough — the choice is yours. What matters is meeting the brief, but keep in mind that things aren’t always what they seem, and rules are made to be broken.

What should I bring?

A laptop, tablet or lap for you to design, code and build on, your team members, and a sack of patience.

What are the judging criteria?

You need to please the crowd. The person or team whom the crowd (and the Angry Client) feel has best met the brief will be the winner. We’ll also award points for humour and originality — lots of points.

Will we win anything?

Fame and glory will be yours along with a shiny gold award and all the Playdough you can eat*. Your name will be up on Gather’s Angry Client page for all to see, and our Angry Client might even hire you. Not that you’d want that. At all.

* Playdough supply will depend on availability at the local dairy. Note that Gather conference does not support the eating of Playdough or indeed any other non-food items.
