Schedule

The Gather 2014 schedule is now available to view or edit. Come along and host a session!

That’s right, you’re in charge of the day’s agenda. Without your input, Gather 2014 is just a bunch of clever people eating lunch and drinking coffee. Actually, that sounds pretty cool … but we think it’ll be better with talks too. :-)

The Sessions

Gather is the conference that’s made for you, by you. You have free rein to do absolutely anything you want – within reason and provided it adheres to our Gatherly Behaviour Policy. Sessions can be show-and-tells, discussions, lessons, or even just a sit-down-and-make-stuff.

You don’t have to be an expert to host a session. You also don’t have to host on your own – suggest a topic and see if anyone is interested in helping you run it. Sessions are not meant to be a series of staid lectures – everyone is encouraged to participate. Stuck for ideas? Here’s what we got up to in 2013 and in 2012.

Hosting a session? Check out our guide: Hosting a session 101.

If you know someone who is doing something great and you think they should be sharing it with a wider audience then encourage them to come along and take part!

Conference keynotes

This is the only point in the day where we go down to a single track and our hand-picked speakers will be presenting in the main auditorium.

 

Lillian Grace

CEO, Wiki New Zealand

Lillian is the CEO and Founder of of Wiki New Zealand, a website working to make data truly accessible to all. They are doing that by collaborating throughout New Zealand’s data ecosystem to create a platform that not only brings all the data together in one place, but that makes it visually explorable. Making the data visual lowers the barriers and transaction costs of engagement, and speeds up the process of generating insight.

@GracefulLillian

Vaughan Rowsell

CEO, Vend

Vaughan is the CEO and Founder of Vend, maker of awesome cloud-based retail software and champions for small to medium retailers worldwide. The Vend team hold three core values dearly to their hearts: to delight retailers, to do the impossible, and to take care of one another. Every year Vaughan personally walks the talk and does an impossible challenge whether it is cycling the length of New Zealand solo, building a $100M venture, or running 1,000 kilometers. He will explain how and why.
